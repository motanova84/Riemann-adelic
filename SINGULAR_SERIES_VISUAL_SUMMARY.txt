## Singular Series Definition

```
For prime p and even n:

         ⎧ 1 - 1/(p-1)²   if p | n
𝔖ₚ(n) = ⎨
         ⎩ 1 + 1/(p-1)³   if p ∤ n


𝔖(n) = ∏ 𝔖ₚ(n)
       p prime
```

### Example: n = 6

```
p=2: 2|6  →  𝔖₂(6) = 1 - 1/1² = 0         [Special case!]
p=3: 3|6  →  𝔖₃(6) = 1 - 1/2² = 3/4
p=5: 5∤6  →  𝔖₅(6) = 1 + 1/4³ = 65/64
p=7: 7∤6  →  𝔖₇(6) = 1 + 1/6³ = 217/216
...

Standard approach: Define 𝔖(n) over odd primes only for even n
```

## Why Some Sorrys Are Expected

From the problem statement:
> "The only sorry that remains is the fine handling of infinite products—
> this is expected and at the frontier of current formal knowledge."

Our 3 sorrys are precisely:
1. Absolute convergence of infinite product ← Requires showing |𝔖ₚ-1| ≪ 1/p²
2. Global positivity from local positivity ← Requires infinite product preservation
3. Explicit lower bounds ← Requires finite truncation + tail estimates

These are **standard results** in analytic number theory textbooks, but require
careful formalization of:
- Product topology and convergence
- Interchange of limits and products
- Effective bounds on infinite tails
